ABSTRACTBACKGROUND:
The accuracy of the GRACE and TIMI scores in predicting coronary disease in with -ST-elevation coronary (ACS) has not been established. OBJECTIVE: To the hypothesis that the GRACE and TIMI risk scores satisfactorily predict coronary disease in with non-ST-elevation ACS undergoing coronary . METHODS: Individuals meeting the objective criteria for ACS and undergoing coronary during hospitalization were consecutively assessed. Angiographic coronary disease was described as follows: quantification of coronary disease by using Gensini score; presence of any (> 70% or > 50% when affecting ); and presence of (three- disease or affecting the main coronary ). RESULTS: Of 112 assessed, a correlation of the Gensini score was observed with the GRACE (p = 0.017) and TIMI (p = 0.02) scores, but that association was weak (r = 0.23 and r = 0.27; respectively). The GRACE score could predict neither obstructive coronary disease (area under the ROC curve = 0.57; 95% CI = 0.46 - 0.69), nor coronary (ROC = 0.59; 95% CI = 0.48 - 0.70). The TIMI score proved to be a modest predictor of coronary disease (ROC = 0.65; 95% CI = 0.55 - 0.76) and of coronary (ROC = 0.66; 95% CI = 0.56 - 0.76).
