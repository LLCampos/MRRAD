ABSTRACT OBJECTIVE:
The aim of this study was to compare estimates of Pap smear and mammogram obtained from household and telephone surveys in women living in São Paulo in 2008, according to sociodemographic characteristics, and to the differences observed from those estimates. METHODS: The study compared data from ISA - Capital 2008, a household survey conducted in São Paulo by Universidade de São Paulo (USP), Universidade Estadual de Campinas (UNICAMP) and the State Health Department with support from the Municipal Health Department, and VIGITEL - São Paulo, a telephone-based Surveillance of Risk and Protective Factors for Diseases. Estimates of Pap smear and , as well as their realization in the year prior to the interview, were compared according to type of survey (household/telephone) by Poisson adjusted for age and . RESULTS: There were statistically significant differences between the estimates obtained by VIGITEL and ISA - Capital as to the prevalence of in the year prior to the interview. However, estimates of the Pap smear at least once in life and in the past year, as well as mammograghy at least once in life, shown statistically significant differences, with higher prevalence rates of coverage among those interviewed by telephone. CONCLUSION: The results indicate a tendency to overestimate coverage of and Pap smear in the telephone survey data, which indicates the need for studies that also contribute to a better understanding of the differences observed by different types of surveys. Population-based surveys are commonly used in epidemiological and represent one of the most traditional study to obtain information on different dimensions related to the health-disease . Due to the recent use of this type of survey in the health field, it is necessary to compare its estimates with those obtained by household surveys, which are more traditional14,18, in to study its validity and information bias. The importance of is reinforced by the advantages presented by telephone surveys, which are cost and speed16,19, enabling the periodic collection of information to monitor risk factors in the population. The objective of this study was to compare estimates of Pap smear and mammogram obtained from household survey (ISA-Capital) and telephone survey (VIGITEL) in women living in São Paulo in 2008, according to sociodemographic characteristics, and to the differences observed from those estimates.   Methods In to compare prevalence estimates of preventive , data from two types of health surveys were used: a household (ISA - Capital 2008) and a telephone survey (VIGITEL - São Paulo 2008). VIGITEL - São Paulo 2008 The telephone-based Surveillance of Risk and Protective Factors for Diseases (VIGITEL) is a monitoring system established by the Ministry of Health and, since 2006, it has interviewed more than 54,000 people a year in the 26 Brazilian and the Federal District by of telephone landline interviews. A probability sample of the population aged 18 years or older was performed in two stages: drawing of telephone and afterward selection of one resident in the household to be interviewed. In 2008, a of 2,013 individuals were interviewed in the city of São Paulo, which enables to estimate the prevalence of risk factors for non-communicable diseases with a 95% coefficient and a error of 2 percentage points17. To obtain information by telephone, a computer was used to immediately read and register the responses of the participants. This questionnaire was comprised of questions regarding demographic and socioeconomic characteristics of the individuals, as well as information on health status, preventive practices, eating habits and physical activity associated with non-communicable diseases17. Sampling weights are attributed to each individual interviewed by VIGITEL in to compensate for the different selection and to decrease bias resulting from non-response and the lack of universal telephone line coverage. Population-based surveys are important to produce information about health10, and the conduction of more studies on strategies adopted to collect data, such as by telephone, household, online and mail surveys, among other possibilities12, are essential. The international experience in the of different methodologies is more documented12,13,25 than in Brazil5. A study conducted in Madrid comparing telephone and to (household) surveys found similarity in 25 out of the 29 analyzed variables13. A study carried out in the United States comparing estimates obtained by telephone (Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance ) and household (National Health Interview Survey) surveys showed similar data as to smoking, weight, , , immunization, health insurance coverage and cost as being limiting factors to health care12. Davis et al.25 compared the prevalence of considering telephone (BRFSS) and household (NHIS) North-American telephone surveys in the periods of 1997-1999 and 2000-2003 and, as in the study, they observed that estimates originated by telephone were statistically higher than those originated by household surveys. The authors also pointed to the importance and suggested a model that could combine information and generate estimates coming from types of surveys. By comparing estimates obtained by VIGITEL 2007 and those coming from the National Household Survey (PNAD - 2003) Viacava, Souza-Junior and Moreira5 observed that VIGITEL estimates were higher in the analyzed , with up to 9 percentage points of differences. Such differences were attributed to the fact that the population analyzed by the telephone survey has access to telephone and belongs to higher socioeconomic levels. By analyzing the -stratification effect on the correction of data obtained by a subpopulation that had a residential telephone line in 2003 for the population, Segri et al.23 observed that, in relation to , the adjustment by , age and schooling was to properly adjust estimate bias. The access to the Pap smear in the life of women aged 20 years or more in this study, despite the statistically significant difference between surveys, was higher than 90%, and as to having had the exam in the year prior to the interview, higher than 50%. Data from 2003 in the city of São Paulo showed similar values for the access throughout life (87.1%) and in the year (57.5%) for women in the same age groups23,26. Another household study conducted in São Paulo in 2000 showed prevalence rates (86.1%) of being examined at least once for women aged between 15 and 49 years; however, the coverage in the year was higher (65.5%) for the same age group27. A household survey conducted in the city of Campinas in 2003 showed that the access to Pap smear was 83.3% for women aged 40 years or more9, and data from PNAD 2008 showed that in Brazil, the access to women aged 25 to 59 years reached 84.5%, to 90.0% in the Southeast region28. The differences found in this study in relation to the age of women and schooling may result from the different of -stratification adjustments used for the analyzed surveys. The fact that ISA - Capital uses data from 2008, while VIGITEL uses data from 2000 - when the population was younger and with fewer years of schooling - emphasizes the differences between these variables. In relation to the differences found as to marital status and especially /ethnicity, the same observation may be considered. It is important to emphasize the importance of using statistical analysis techniques, especially Poisson models18, in data from surveys gathered in one databank. This combination enabled the use of computational analysis modules which consider the different aspects of the sample plans, which differ in surveys. It is important to mention that the between prevalence estimates was conducted by the prevalence ratio adjusted by age and schooling, once the surveys refer to populations with different percentage distributions as to these variables, because -stratification adjustments at VIGITEL were made based on Census 2000, and ISA - Capital was based on PNAD 2008. The results of this study indicate the tendency to overestimate some indicators of and Pap smear coverage in the telephone survey data, which indicates the need for studies that bias and its correction by -stratification adjustments that contribute to a better understanding of the differences observed by different types of surveys. Thus, estimates obtained by telephone can continue to guide the planning of health promotion policies in the country.
