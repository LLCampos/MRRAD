ABSTRACT BACKGROUND:
GRACE and TIMI's accuracy scores to predict the extent of disease in with coronary ST- elevation (ACS) is not established. To test the hypothesis of the risk scores, GRACE and TIMI satisfactorily predict the extent of disease in with SCA undergoing coronary . METHODS: Individuals admitted to objective criteria of SCA who underwent coronary during hospitalization were consecutively analyzed. The angiographic coronary disease was described in three ways: quantification of the extent of disease by the Gensini's score; presence of any coronary (> 70% or > 50% when the ); presence of (three- or main coronary ). RESULTS: In 112 evaluated, there was a correlation of Gensini's score with GRACE's scores (p = 0.017) and TIMI (p = 0.02), but this association was of a weak magnitude (r = 0.23 and r = 0.27, respectively). The GRACE score was not able to predict obstructive (area under the ROC curve = 0.57; 95% CI = 0.46 to 0.69), or (ROC = 0.59; 95% CI = 0.48 -0.70). The TIMI score showed a modest predictor for the presence of disease (ROC = 0.65; 95% CI = 0.55 to 0.76) and the presence of (ROC = 0.66; 95% CI = 0, 56-.76). CONCLUSION: (1) There is a association between the value of TIMI or GRACE scores and extent of coronary disease in with SCA; (2) However, the degree of this association is not enough so that these scores become predictors of the coronary accurate results. Thus, the risk stratification is essential for proper clinical decision discriminating individuals who benefit from more strategies. It has been shown that the use of multivariate models in the form of scores represent the most accurate for predicting risk, higher than the subjectively obtained by clinical impression2. The TIMI and GRACE scores are the most used and have prognostic value established by prospectiva 1,3 cohort studies. Besides the prognostic , the prediction of the anatomical extent of the disease is potentially useful for clinical decision. This study does not confirm the initial hypothesis that the TIMI and GRACE scores predict accurately the presence and anatomic extent of coronary disease in with ACS. In this work, the coronary anatomy was described as quantitative variable (Gensini score) and as a categorical variable (presence of obstructive or ). In the case, there was a statistically significant correlation of the anatomy with scores. This trend is confirmed by the dose-response gradient between the tertiles of the scores and the value of the Gensini score, statistically significant in the case of TIMI and borderline in the case of GRACE. These analyzes suggest that the prognostic information contained in scores is mediated by the extent of the coronary anatomy. When, in the meanwhile, the analysis of the strength of association of these scores is made with coronary anatomy, the results go in another direction. , the weak correlation (r < 0.30) with the Gensini score shows that the calibration of prognostic scores to predict coronary anatomy is . That is, using the formula for the extent of the disease, there is a great difference between the predicted value and the observed value of the Gensini score. , there is a reasonable overlap of the IIQ of the Gensini score between the terciles of GRACE and TIMI. , the area under the ROC curve of the GRACE score is not statistically significant and the TIMI score, although statistically significant, reveals modest accuracy in the discrimination of with obstructive disease or (area < 0.70). These data indicate that the prognostic scores did not play a role as predictive models of the coronary anatomy. It is that prognosis of the is directly influenced by the extent of involvement . The answer to this question comes from the fact that the anatomical extent of the is only one of determinants of severity in a multivariate model , of most biological systems. That is, the prognosis depends not only on the anatomy. Thus, these scores may be able to predict risk necessarily predict anatomy in the same proportion. The GRACE score has prognostic value when compared to the TIMI score in different studies2,5,13. Paradoxically, TIMI showed better prediction of the anatomy. This finding may be explained by the fact that the TIMI score considers some variables specifically related to the existence of coronary disease 1. For example, one of the TIMI variables is exactly the prior knowledge that the has obstructive disease; moreover, prior use of aspirin and many risk factors, unique variables of TIMI, more coronary disease. The practical implications of the results should be discussed. The two scores are commonly used on clinical decision of ACS , in to define the of the anti-thrombotic and the stratification strategy, or 14.15. This utility is grounded in established prognostic value of these scores and in the relationship of risk with the magnitude of the benefit of strategies. It's important to note that our results do not contradict the value of scores in clinical decision. However, we must recognize that the usefulness of these would be higher if they were able to predict anatomy.
