ABSTRACT BACKGROUND:
The accuracy of the GRACE and TIMI scores to predict the extent of disease in with coronary ST- elevation (ACS) is not established. To test the hypothesis that the risk scores GRACE and TIMI satisfactorily predict the extent of disease in with ACS undergoing coronary . METHODS: Individuals admitted to objective criteria of ACS who underwent coronary during hospitalization were consecutively analyzed. The angiographic coronary disease was described in three ways: quantification of the extent of disease by Gensini score; presence of any coronary ( > 70% or > 50% when the ); presence of (three- or main coronary ). RESULTS: In 112 evaluated, there was a correlation of Gensini score with GRACE scores (p = 0.017) and TIMI (p = 0.02), but this association was weak magnitude (r = 0.23 and r = 0.27, respectively). The GRACE score was not able to predict obstructive (area under the ROC curve = 0.57; 95% CI = 0.46 to 0.69), or (ROC = 0.59; 95% CI = 0.48 -0.70). The TIMI showed modest predictor for the presence of disease (ROC = 0.65; 95% CI = 0.55 to 0.76) and the presence of (ROC = 0.66; 95% CI = 0 , 56-.76). CONCLUSION: (1) There is a association between the value of TIMI or GRACE scores and extent of coronary disease in with ACS; (2) However, the degree of this association is not enough that these scores are predictors of coronary accurate results.
