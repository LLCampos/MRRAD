ABSTRACT BACKGROUND:
Although some evidence an association between exposure technology in the scientific literature and its dissemination in clinical practice, studies have examined this association. The aim of this study is to analyze the pattern of scientific publication on two competitive technologies used in the evaluation of disease (CAD) reflects what occurs in clinical practice. METHODS: We evaluated the number of scientific articles published each year in the medical literature ( scientific interest in technology) on two technologies used in the evaluation of disease: and electron beam with detectors. It was also analyzed the number of countries that publish annual scientific articles about these technologies ( interest in technology). RESULTS: electron beam (electron beam computed tomography - EBCT) showed peak of " scientific interest" in 2001, with 127 published articles. this peak, the " scientific interest" by about 50% in 2008. In contrast, the " scientific interest" by with detectors ( - MDCT) progressively until 2007, with 454 articles published in that year. The " scientific interest" by EBCT had peak in 2002 with 14 countries publishing about this technology. this , " scientific interest" declined by almost 25% by 2008, with countries publishing articles on this technology. In opposition, the " scientific interest" by MDCT progressively until 2008, with 37 countries publishing articles on it. CONCLUSION: The medical literature supports the substitution of EBCT by MDCT in the evaluation of CAD.
