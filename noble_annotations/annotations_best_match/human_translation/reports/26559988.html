Discussion CAD phenotyping by CCTA has a relevant prognostic impact and risk classification for cardiovascular events when compared to the classic risk factors11- 14. This study allowed to evaluate, in the real world and in the term, the impact of CCTA results on drug , evaluating trends in cholesterol levels and the use of shortly the CCTA results. The analysis of this impact on clinical is relevant, as the benefit of antiplatelet agents and CLD is directly associated with the cardiovascular risk. It has been demonstrated that in an population with zero , the NNT with aspirin is approximately two thousand individuals to prevent one cardiovascular event, while the number needed to harm (NNH) is 442 individuals, demonstrating a much greater risk in the prescription of aspirin than its benefits15. A cost-effectiveness analysis showed that the aspirin is only cost-effective in men with clinical risk in ten years greater than 10% and in women when the risk is > 15%16. In our study, aspirin was started in 5% of a completely CCTA result, which would be indicated, considering that the annual risk is < 0.1% of combined coronary events9, 16, 17. Conversely, we observed that 22% of with CCTA (obstructive or not) in our study started aspirin . The proportion of that initiated with CLD has significantly as the severity of CAD ( CCTA = 12%; non-obstructive CAD = 24%; CAD ≥ 50% = 43%), demonstrating that in the real world there is an agreement between intensity and the severity of CAD .
